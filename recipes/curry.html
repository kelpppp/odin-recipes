Japanese Chicken Curry

Description

Japanese Curry Rice is a well-loved comfort food, with bite-sized chunks of meat, thick-cut vegetables, and rich curry sauce.

The curry has a stew-like consistency and includes chicken, onions, carrots, and potatoes. The sauce is thickened by a roux - a mix of fat, flour, and curry spices.

Ingredients

  • 1 onion
  • 1 carrot
  • 1.5 Yukon gold potatoes
  • 0.5 tsp grated ginger
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 0.25 apple
  • 340 g boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • ground black pepper
  • 0.75 tbsp cooking oil
  • 480 ml chicken broth
  • 0.5 tbsp honey
  • 0.5 tbsp soy sauce
  • 0.5 tbsp ketchup
  • 0.5 package Japanese curry roux
  • 4 servings cooked short-grain rice

Steps

  1. Cut onion into wedges.
  2. Peel carrot and cut them into rolling wedges.
  3. Peel potatoes and cut them into quarters. Soak in water to remove excess starch.
  4. Grate ginger and mince garlic clove.
  5. Cut and grate apple slice.
  6. Cut off extra fat from chicken thighs. Cut into bit sized pieces and season with black pepper.
  7. Heat cooking oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion.
  8. Saute onions, stirring occasionally, until tender and golden.
  9. Add minced garlic and grated ginger. Mix well.
  10. Add chicken and cook, stirring frequently, until no longer pink.
  11. Add 480 ml chicken broth.
  12. Add grated apple, honey, soy sauce, and ketchup.
  13. Add carrots and potatoes. The broth should barely cover the ingredients.
  14. Simmer covered on medium-low heat for 15 minutes.
  15. Cook until a wooden skewer goes through carrots and potatoes.
  16. Turn off heat. Put 1-2 cubes of curry roux into a ladleful of cooking liquid and let it dissolve.
  17. Repeat until all curry roux blocks are incorporated.
  18. Simmer uncovered on medium-low heat, stirring frequently, until the curry becomes thick.
  19. Serve curry with steamed short-grain rice on the side.
