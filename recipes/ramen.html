Miso Ramen

Description

Ramen is a Japanese adaptations of the Chinese "pulled noodles."

It's a quick and intensely savory dish. The piping-hot bowl of soulful goodness is sure to satisfy your cravings!

Ingredients

  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 0.75 tsp grated ginger
  • 1.5 shallot
  • 1.5 tbsp toasted white sesame seeds
  • 1.5 tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • 170 g ground pork
  • 1.5 tsp doubanjiang - spicy chili bean paste
  • 4.5 tbsp miso
  • 1.5 tbsp sugar
  • 1.5 tbsp sake
  • 1440 ml chicken broth
  • 1.5 tsp kosher salt
  • 0.4 tsp white pepper powder
  • 3 servings fresh ramen noodles
  • toppings:
  • tonkatsu slices
  • 3 ramen eggs
  • blanched bean sprouts
  • canned corn
  • nori seaweed
  • green onion

Steps

  1. Mince garlic cloves, shallots, and grate ginger.
  2. Grind sesame seeds.
  3. Heat medium pot over medium-lot heat.
  4. Add sesame oil and garlic, ginger, and shallot. Stir-fry until fragrant.
  5. Add ground pork and increase to medium heat. Cook until no longer pink.
  6. Add doubanjiang and miso. Quickly blend with meat without burning.
  7. Add ground sesame seeds and sugar. Mix well.
  8. Add sake and chicken broth. Bring the mixture to a simmer.
  9. Add salt and pepper powder to taste.
  10. Bring a large pot of water to boil.
  11. Separate fresh ramen noodles with hand to loosen.
  12. Cook noodles according to package instructions, until al-dente.
  13. Pick up noodles with mesh sieve and drain excess water
  14. Add ramen soup broth to each bowl. Place toppings of choice and serve.
